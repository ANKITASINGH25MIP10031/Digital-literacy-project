## UPI/ONLINE PAYMENT FRAUD
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of cybercrime where attackers deceive people into making payments using mobile payment apps. Since digital transactions have expanded exponentially in India, this kind of fraud is becoming quite common.

In a UPI fraud scam, an attacker calls the victim by phone, sends an SMS, or contacts them using instant messaging services. The fraudster may pose as a bank official, a delivery executive, or a customer support representative. In other incidents, the victim may be sent a payment request link or a QR code. The victim is convinced that making a payment by accepting the request or scanning the code will lead them to receive money. Instead, by doing this, the victim may be duped into authorizing a payment from their own account, which leads to an amount being deducted.

These scams often occur with students, the elderly or people who are new to digital payments. Attackers often exploit the victim's fear or confusion, hurrying them to finish their transaction before they can notice anything suspicious.

The impact of a UPI fraud can be a direct financial loss, psychological trauma and it can sometimes be a major problem to get the money back, while victims might even lose faith in online payment platforms.

To avoid such fraud, users must never share their OTP, PIN, or other personal details and must refrain from scanning any unrecognized QR codes or approving any unexpected payment requests. Vigilance and careful attention to detail is important.
